Spots

New York City is a densly populated city with various iconic sites. However, navigating in New York City can be stressful as it is filled with tourists and New Yorkers who trying to go on with their day. Therefore, I have composed my 5 underrated spots you can visit in NYC.

1. Rooftop Park - Pier 57
This is a free rooftop park located by the Hudson River. It provides amazing views of Lower Manhattan, mid-town Manhattan, and New Jersey. It is the best time to go at sunset so you can see NYC sunsets. Additionally, Chelsea Market is located nearby which you can get a lot of great food. Some other sites like Highline Park, Little Island, and the Vessel are nearby.
2. Battery Park
This park is located at the southern tip of Manhattan. In this park, you can enjoy the views of the Hudson River, East River, Statue of Liberty, Governor's Island, and Lower Manhattan. Additionally, you can ride the Staten Island Ferry for free which is in Battery Park. There are also many food trucks there where you can try out various hotdogs and burgers.
3. Roosevelt Island
This island is located between Manhattan and Queens and bounded by the East River. There are various shops in teh island and a graduate campus which is owned by Cornell University. The most special thing about this island is the tram you can ride from Manhattan to Roosevelt. The tram offers spontaneous views of midtown Manhattan and the Williamsburg Bridge.
4. Shore Road Parkway
This spot is located in Bay Ridge, Southern Brooklyn. This is a trail along the river and allows you to walk or bike through it while looking at the views of the Verazzano Bridge, Staten Island, and New York Bay. There are various parks near by including Owl Head's Park and Shore Road Park, including a monument and pier where you can take the ferry. Walking further down, you will reach Ceasar's Bay shopping center which has a lot shops and restaurants there.
5. 34th Street Herald Sq
This spot located in Midtown Manhattan has various shops, like H&M, Macy, Sephora, etc. It also has a lot of restaurant. A popular site, Korea Town, is located there and it has a lot of delicious Korean food, including BBQ, hotpot, tofu, and noodles. This also has connections to the world's famous Madison Square Garden, Penn Station, and Time Square.
